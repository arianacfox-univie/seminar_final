The main party, along with most of the Shoshone, crosses back into Idaho, across the Continental Divide.

Clark informs Lewis of the unsatisfactory reconnaissance of the Salmon River and lists three possible crossing of the mountains. He “advised the purchase of horses and the prusute of a rout he had learned from his guid who had promised to piolet ous to the road north &c.”
