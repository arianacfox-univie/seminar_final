Aug. 18, 1805
Part of the expedition spends a week at the junction of Horse Prairie Creek and Red River Rock (Camp Fortunate).

Clark and 11 men leave camp Fortunate to “examine the Country” and to find timber to build canoes to take them to the Pacific Ocean. Most of the Indians leave with Clark. Sacagewea and Charbonneau accompany Clark as far as the Shoshone village.
